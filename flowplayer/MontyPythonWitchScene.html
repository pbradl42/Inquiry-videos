Monty Python - Witch Scene In perhaps the most famous scene from Monty Python's most famous movie, a mob is lead through the basic reasoning of verificationism: first, consider the conditions of knowledge ("How do you know that she is a witch"), then produce a theory ("Witches burn"), deduce a prediction ("If she will burn, she is made of wood, therefore she'll float. And if she floats, then she's lighter than a duck.") Check to see if the prediction is confirmed, and burn away!
